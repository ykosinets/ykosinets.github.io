About

Beamiie studies marine and freshwater environments, ecosystems, and the changing relationship between people and aquatic environments.

Our work begins with close observation in the field. By following how water, wildlife, climate, and communities interact over time, we build a clearer picture of the pressures shaping aquatic places.

We believe useful environmental knowledge should be rigorous, accessible, and connected to the people responsible for protecting these systems.

Our team brings together field researchers, data specialists, and environmental practitioners. This mix of perspectives helps us connect detailed scientific evidence with the wider conditions affecting each place.

We work collaboratively with local partners, institutions, and communities throughout the research process—from defining the right questions to sharing findings that can guide monitoring, restoration, and long-term stewardship.

Research

We combine observation, data, and environmental science to support healthier waters and stronger knowledge of aquatic ecosystems.

Our research spans field sampling, long-term environmental monitoring, ecological assessment, and the interpretation of physical and biological data from oceans, lakes, and connected waterways.

Each project is designed to answer practical questions: how environments are changing, what is driving that change, and where focused action can have the greatest impact.
